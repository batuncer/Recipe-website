Lox Bagels

Traditionally, bagels are not made with milk, hence vejahn. Sometimes, however, the water is replaced entirely with milk for a more brioche-like consistency. But we don't want that!

  • 8 people
  • 30 min

Ingredients

  • 540 grams of flour
  • 355 grams of water
  • 1 packet of yeast
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tablespoons sugar for the cooking water
  • Sesame, poppy, zaatar, u name it
  • 1 egg or some soy milk for brushing

Coking instructions

Step 1
If you want to do it with Saui:
Step 2
The day before, mix 25 g starter, 50 g water and 50 g flour into the pre-dough. Mix with the water the next day, just add 2 g of yeast. Reduce the amount of flour and water from the list above accordingly.
Step 3
Dissolve the yeast and sugar in warm water and leave to foam for 10-15 minutes at room temperature. Add flour and salt and knead into a dough until it is no longer sticky. Add a little more flour if you need it, but it should come from kneading. Cover and leave to stand for 40-60 minutes until the dough has doubled in size.
Step 4
Divide into 8 pieces of about the same size, shape into a ball and poke a hole in the middle. Put a chopstick or wooden spoon in the hole and swing the bagels quickly to enlarge the hole. Cover and let rise again for 20-30 minutes.
Step 5
Let them soak in boiling water for 2 minutes on each side and place on baking paper. Spread with Eggwash and top as desired. In the oven at 200 degrees for 20-25 minutes. Finished! The best bleach is simply lox & cream cheese
