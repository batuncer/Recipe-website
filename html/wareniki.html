Wareniki

Varenyky are popular across the globe, and abroad they have other names, shapes, and fillings. A traditional Ukrainian varenyk is twice bigger than a russian pelmen and four times larger in size than an Italian ravioli. Ukrainian varenyky are half moon shaped, russian pelmeni have a round form, and Italian ravioli have mostly a square shape. In Poland varenyky are called kalduny and their recipes are similar to Ukrainian ones.

  • 2 people
  • 40 min

Ingredients

  • 200g flour
  • 60g water/li>
  • 1 egg, optionally a little more water
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • Filling:
  • Kapu
  • 1 onion, sweated
  • Salt pepper

Coking instructions

Step 1
Knead a firm dough and let it rest for 30 minutes. For the filling, boil potatoes, mash, sauté onions in butter, pack and allow to cool. Season well, don't forget. It's not actually part of the recipe, but I'll add a dollop of mustard to Herne's mash.
Step 2
Roll out the dough thinly, cut out circles with a glass and fold Wareniki. To do this, simply fold the circles in the middle around the filling and press the edges together firmly.
Step 3
In addition, sauté an onion (if you already have it) in butter and chop vegan bacon or smoked tofu into small pieces.
