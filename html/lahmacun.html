Lahmacun

Lahmacun is a traditional snack from Turkey - but also from parts of Armenia. Here's a relatively simple vegan version. Instead of meat substitutes, you can also use simply cooked lentils, which are seasoned in exactly the same way and pressed into a paste.

  • 4 people
  • 20 min

Ingredients

  • 500-550g flour
  • 180 ml oat milk
  • 100ml of water
  • 100 grams of olive oil
  • 7 gr dry yeast OR
  • 1 cube of fresh yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar/li>
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Filling:
  • 500g vegan mince (mine is from Greenforce)
  • 70g olive oil
  • 3 onions
  • 2 small ox heart tomatoes or 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 2 red pointed peppers
  • 1 pointed pepper green
  • 2 tbsp paprika paste
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • half a bunch of parsley
  • 1 tsp chilli flakes
  • some salt and pepper
  • optional: dried mint and oregano

Coking instructions

Step 1
The basis for the lahmacun is a thin yeast dough. To do this, dissolve the yeast and sugar in a warm mixture of milk and water and leave to rise at room temperature for about 15 minutes. Then add the remaining ingredients. Knead for at least 10-15 minutes to form a smooth dough, cover and let rise in a warm place for 40-60 minutes.
Step 2
Meanwhile, prepare the filling: grate all the vegetables or put them through a processor and mix well with the minced meat along with the spices. It'll be a little runny at first, but the vegan mince draws in a lot of the liquid. Just let it stand for 15 minutes until the mass gets a good consistency.
Step 3
Divide the dough into 10 parts, form into balls and cover. Roll out the dough pieces thinly one after the other, spread thinly with the minced mixture. Bake in a coated or oiled pan with the lid on for 5-7 minutes over medium heat. If you don't have a pan with a lid, you can also use it in the oven at 200Â°. Once fried, the lahmacun is stacked like this: minced side to minced side, batter side to batter side.
Step 4
Top the lahmacun with the fresh lettuce, roll & scoop, I like yoghurt with it, and more cucumbers than tomatoes in the salad. But you do as you like.
